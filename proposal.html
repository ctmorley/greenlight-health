Clinical Data Strategy: Payer Rules & Criteria Sources

The clinical rules engine is GreenLight's core IP. This section maps the authoritative data sources that power payer-specific PA determination, denial prevention, and appeal generation.

Tiered Data Acquisition Strategy

Tier 1 — Public Clinical Standards (Free)

Foundational clinical correctness layer. Available immediately, no licensing required.

  • ACR Appropriateness Criteria: ~200+ clinical conditions mapped to imaging modalities. Each topic rated 1–9 (usually appropriate / may be appropriate / usually not appropriate). Machine-readable via ACR API at acsearch.acr.org
  • CMS NCDs & LCDs: National & Local Coverage Determinations define exactly what Medicare covers and what requires PA. Published in structured formats at cms.gov
  • CMS Annual PA List: Medicare Advantage prior authorization requirements published annually — definitive source for MA plans
No costImmediate accessMachine-readable

Tier 2 — RBM Published Guidelines (Free)

The actual criteria that determine approval/denial for most commercial plans. Published as PDFs, parseable with AI.

  • EviCore (Evernorth/Cigna): Clinical guidelines at evicore.com/provider, organized by specialty — radiology, cardiology, oncology, musculoskeletal
  • Carelon (Elevance/Anthem): Clinical appropriateness guidelines at carelonmedicalbenefits.com — covers imaging, radiation therapy, sleep studies
  • NIA / Evolent Health: National Imaging Associates guidelines on provider portal — primarily advanced imaging (MRI, CT, PET, nuclear medicine)
No costPDF parsing requiredHighest denial-prevention value

Tier 3 — Payer-Specific Policies (Free, Manual)

Individual payer PA requirements by CPT code, plan type, and state. Scattered across provider portals.

  • UnitedHealthcare: uhcprovider.com → Prior Authorization & Notification lookup tool
  • Aetna: aetna.com/health-care-professionals → Precertification lists by plan
  • Cigna: cignaforhcp.cigna.com → Coverage Policies searchable by CPT
  • BCBS: Varies by state plan — each publishes a medical policy manual with PA requirements
  • Humana, Anthem, Centene: Provider portals with PA lookup tools
No costHigh maintenanceChanges without notice

Tier 4 — Licensed Clinical Criteria (Commercial)

Industry-standard clinical decision support used by payers themselves to adjudicate PA requests.

  • InterQual (Cotiviti): Industry-standard criteria used by major payers. Structured, machine-readable criteria sets. Covers imaging appropriateness, surgical necessity, and level-of-care
  • MCG (Milliman Care Guidelines): Used by ~2,700 hospitals and health plans. Covers imaging, surgical, and procedure appropriateness with evidence-based indications
Commercial licenseAuthoritativeMachine-readable

Why This Is the Moat

The rules engine is the product. The platform UI and workflow automation are table stakes. The real value is knowing — for a specific payer, plan, state, CPT code, and clinical scenario — exactly what documentation is needed, what criteria must be met, and what language will get an approval. This knowledge compound over time as GreenLight processes more PAs and learns from outcomes.
Keeping rules current is the moat. Payers change PA requirements frequently and often without notice. EviCore updates clinical guidelines semi-annually. State Medicaid programs change quarterly. Building a system that continuously monitors, parses, and ingests these changes — before they cause denials — is what separates GreenLight from horizontal PA tools.

Why Imaging/Surgical PAs Resist Automation

Unstructured Clinical Documentation

Requires narrative clinical notes, prior imaging results, failed conservative treatment docs, and lab values — scattered across EHR systems. Pharmacy PAs use structured formulary data.

Payer Criteria Variability

Each payer uses different criteria (InterQual, MCG, proprietary). Same MRI approved by one insurer, denied by another for identical patients. No standardization.

Peer-to-Peer Review Burden

Unique to medical/surgical PAs. Physicians must schedule phone calls with insurance medical directors. ASTRO documents reviewers lacking relevant specialty expertise.

Multiple RBM Gatekeepers

EviCore, Carelon, and NIA each have different portals, rules, and workflows. Payers switch RBMs without notice — breaking provider workflows overnight.

PACS/RIS Integration Needed

Imaging PAs require connection to radiology-specific systems, not just EHRs. Most horizontal PA companies don't have this capability.

Scheduling Cascade Impact

Delayed surgical PA disrupts OR schedules, anesthesia, patient prep, and downstream appointments — a multi-party coordination problem pharmacy PA doesn't face.

The damning statistic: 80.7% of appealed Medicare Advantage PA denials were overturned in 2024 (KFF). Some insurers exceed 95% overturn rates. But only 11.5% of denials are actually appealed. The system works as a friction-based cost reduction mechanism — most denials are effectively wrong, but payers profit from providers who don't fight back. An AI that gets submissions right the first time attacks this directly.

APPENDIX A5

Strategic Assessment: Bull Case vs. Bear Case

An honest, data-driven analysis of whether the imaging/surgery PA niche is worth a multi-year commitment.

The Bull Case

1. Massive, Proven Market

$35–56B in annual waste. $1.5–30M revenue leakage per facility. 94% of physicians say PA harms care. Pain is real and quantified.

2. Clear Competitive Whitespace

CoverMyMeds solved pharmacy PA. No equivalent exists for medical/imaging/surgical PA. The "CoverMyMeds for medical PA" position is genuinely available.

3. Regulatory Tailwind with Hard Deadline

CMS-0057 forces payers to build FHIR APIs by Jan 2027 — creating standardized infrastructure that GreenLight builds on. Timing is excellent.

4. Complexity = Defensibility

Unstructured clinical docs, payer variability, RBM fragmentation, PACS integration create genuine moats. Rules engine compounds over time with more PA outcomes data.

5. Unit Economics Work

$8/PA with 3–4x customer ROI. Performance guarantee de-risks adoption. Revenue per customer scales from $77K/yr (imaging center) to $4.8M/yr (multi-hospital).

6. Validated by Market Signals

Cohere Health ($5.5B valuation) proves PA AI is valued. 94% of payers plan substantial AI-PA investment. 67% plan to reevaluate vendors by 2026. Demand is surging.

The Bear Case & Mitigations

1. EHR Vendors Build Native PA

Epic already has some PA features. Mitigation: EHR tools will be generic; imaging-specific intelligence (ACR criteria, PACS/RIS, RBM navigation) is too specialized for EHR platforms. Partner, don't compete.

2. Olive AI Cautionary Tale

$400M raised at $4B valuation, collapsed in 2023. Mitigation: Olive failed from lack of focus ("automate everything"). GreenLight's vertical niche strategy is the opposite approach.

3. CMS-0057 Could Reduce PA Volume

Faster decisions + transparency may eliminate some PAs. Mitigation: MA PA volume has actually grown from 37M (2019) to 53M (2024) despite reform talk. Payers add PAs faster than they remove them.

4. Customer Concentration Risk

15 customers in Year 1 creates dependency. Mitigation: GTM plan diversifies across facility types (imaging centers → ASCs → hospitals). Enforce no single customer >15% of revenue.

5. Payer Portal Instability

Payers change portals, rules, and RBMs without notice. Mitigation: CMS-0057 API mandate reduces portal dependency. Dedicated monitoring team required as permanent OpEx.

VERDICT This is not a mirage. The convergence of massive pain ($35B+ waste), clear whitespace (no CoverMyMeds for medical PA), and a regulatory catalyst (CMS-0057, Jan 2027) makes this a rare setup. The key risk is execution, not market validity. Worth going all-in.
